Glossary

Terms that appear throughout the course, in one searchable place.

Game theory

Alpha

The fold frequency required from the defender to make a bluff break even. Formula: bet / (pot + bet). A pot-sized bluff needs 50% folds (alpha = 50%).

Blocker

A card in your hand that reduces the number of specific combos villain can hold. Holding the A♠ on a three-spade board blocks the nut flush.

Equilibrium (Nash)

A strategy pair where neither player can unilaterally deviate to improve their EV. The defining concept of GTO.

Equity

Your share of the pot at showdown, averaged over all future runouts. A coin flip has 50% equity.

EV (Expected Value)

The long-run average result of a decision, weighted by probability.

Exploitative play

Deviating from GTO to target a specific opponent leak. Unbalanced, higher-EV against the targeted population, exploitable against someone who counter-adjusts.

GTO (Game Theory Optimal)

A strategy at Nash equilibrium — it cannot be exploited by any opponent, though it may not maximally exploit any particular opponent.

Indifference

The condition where a specific hand's EV is equal across two or more actions. Indifference enables mixed strategies at equilibrium.

MDF (Minimum Defense Frequency)

The minimum frequency you must continue (call or raise) against a bet to prevent a pure 0%-equity bluff from being profitable. Formula: pot / (pot + bet).

Mixed strategy

A strategy where a specific hand takes different actions with specified probabilities (e.g., 4-bet 30%, call 50%, fold 20%).

Node-locking

Fixing a specific frequency or range in a solver to model an opponent's known deviation, then re-solving to find the optimal counter-strategy.

Polar (polarized) range

A range composed of very strong hands and bluffs, with no middle. Typical of large-bet lines on the river.

Range advantage

Having higher average equity than villain's range on a given flop.

Nut advantage

Having more combinations of the absolute strongest hands (sets, straights, flushes) on a flop, independent of overall equity.

Cash concepts

3-bet

The third raise preflop (posted blind → open → 3-bet). By extension, a re-raise of any preflop raise.

4-bet, 5-bet

The fourth and fifth raises preflop. 5-bet is usually all-in at 100bb.

C-bet (continuation bet)

A flop bet by the preflop aggressor.

Check-raise

Checking with the intent to raise when villain bets. A polar tool, usually reserved for strong hands and select bluffs.

Donk bet

A bet by an OOP player who was not the preflop aggressor. Rarely optimal at equilibrium but appears in some solver solutions.

Equity realization

The percentage of your raw equity you actually capture at showdown. Hands that can be bluffed off the pot realize less equity; nuts realize 100%.

Float

Calling a c-bet with a weak hand, intending to win the pot with aggression on a later street.

Iso(late)

Raising over a limp to isolate the limper and play heads-up postflop.

Overbet

A bet larger than the pot, used for polar value and bluffs when nut advantage is extreme.

PFR (Preflop Raiser)

The player who made the last preflop raise and takes initiative postflop.

Range bet

C-betting with your entire range at a small size. Used when range advantage is large.

Range c-bet

Synonym for range bet.

RFI (Raise First In)

The action of opening the pot unopened. Your RFI range is position-dependent.

Squeeze

A 3-bet after an open and at least one caller. Leverages dead money plus multiple fold candidates.

Tournament-specific

Bubble

The point just before a pay-out threshold. The money bubble is the most notable; pay-jump bubbles occur throughout the final table.

Chip EV

EV measured in tournament chips, ignoring ICM.

$EV (dollar EV)

EV measured in prize-pool dollars, using ICM or FGS to convert chip stacks.

Covered / covers

Player A covers player B if A has more chips than B. A can eliminate B.

FGS (Future Game Simulation)

An extension of ICM that models N additional hands of play before applying payout equity. More accurate for skill-adjusted equity.

ICM (Independent Chip Model)

A model that converts chip stacks to expected prize-pool dollars by calculating probabilities of each finishing position.

Iso shove

Re-shoving all-in over an opener to isolate and take down the pot with fold equity + equity when called.

Ladder

Moving up pay positions without busting. Short stacks "ladder" when other players bust first.

Nash range

The push or call range derived from the Nash equilibrium of the two-player push/fold subgame. Chip-EV only unless specified.

Open shove

Going all-in as the first raiser preflop. Standard short-stack action.

Pay jump

The difference in prize money between adjacent finishing positions.

Risk premium

The extra equity required for a $EV-breakeven call above the chip-EV-breakeven point. Scales with pay jumps and stack asymmetry.

Stack sizes and math

bb (big blind)

The standard unit of measurement. 100bb = deep, 10bb = short.

Effective stack

The smaller of your stack and villain's stack. Dictates the real decision tree.

SPR (Stack-to-Pot Ratio)

The ratio of remaining stack to the current pot. Governs geometric sizing and commitment thresholds.

Pot odds

The price you're being offered to call: call / (pot + call). Required equity to break even.

Geometric sizing

Sizing each street so that the stack is committed by the river. Dividing the remaining pot into equal geometric increments.
