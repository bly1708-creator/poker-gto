Preflop Opening Ranges by Position

Position changes your range by a factor of 3. Here's why, and how to memorize each seat's 100bb RFI.

The opening structure at 100bb

Modern solver solutions for 6-max NL at 100bb with typical rake open to 2.5bb from every position. The range tightens as you get further from the button because:

  • The number of players left to act is a geometric multiplier on how often you'll face a 3-bet.
  • You are more likely to be out of position postflop.
  • Rake is a fixed drag on small edges — marginal opens become losers earlier.

RFI ranges — all six positions

Click a position tab to see its open-raise chart. The grid is the standard 13×13: pairs on the diagonal, suited below, offsuit above. Red = open, half-red = mix, empty = fold.

When each hand enters the range

One chart, 169 hands: each cell is colored by the earliest position that opens it. Dark red = UTG-wide (premium). Gold = BTN-only. Empty = never opened from any position. Quick way to see which hands are "positional" and which are universal.

Sizing: why 2.5bb, not 3bb

2.5bb is the smallest opening size that still denies the blinds a profitable call with their entire defense range. Larger sizings (3bb+) give up EV because:

  • You risk more chips per chip of fold equity gained.
  • You make 3-bets cheaper relative to pot, encouraging light defense.
  • You inflate SPR with your weaker opens, which hurts your range postflop.
Common leak: opening too tight from the BTN. If you're under 40% RFI on the button you are leaving money on the table. The blinds' rake and positional penalty make wide BTN opens one of the most profitable actions in poker.

Small blind: raise or limp?

Against a solver BB, the SB uses a mixed strategy: a ~25% limping range (small pairs, suited connectors, weak Ax) and a ~30% raising range (premiums and polar bluffs). Against a typical human BB that over-defends or folds too much, pure raising with a 40% linear range is usually more profitable. Start with pure-raise; add the limp mix once you have reads.
