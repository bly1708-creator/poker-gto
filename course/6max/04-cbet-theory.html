Continuation Betting Theory

Range advantage, nut advantage, SPR, and why "c-bet every flop" is a decade-old leak.

Two advantages, not one

Whether you c-bet depends on two independent properties of the flop:

  • Range advantage: whose range has more total equity on this flop?
  • Nut advantage: whose range has more of the very best hands (sets, two pair, straights)?

You can have range advantage without nut advantage, and vice versa. Treating them the same is the most common c-bet mistake.

Range c-bet vs polar c-bet

Two distinct sizings, two distinct purposes:

Small c-bet (~25-33% pot) — "range bet"

  • Used when you have a big range advantage.
  • Bets close to your entire range, including air.
  • Denies equity to villain's weak Ax and middling pairs.
  • Minimum defense frequency required from villain: 75%.

Big c-bet (~75-100% pot) — "polar bet"

  • Used when you have nut advantage on a dynamic board.
  • Bets a polarized range: sets + straights + select combo draws as bluffs.
  • Checks everything medium: top pair good kicker, pocket pairs, gutshots.
  • MDF drops to ~57%. Villain folds the middle, calls the top.
Quick diagnostic: if you're c-betting AKx flops pot, you're burning value. Villain's range is too weak to call a big bet; you make more using a small size and betting with your whole range.

SPR and commitment

Stack-to-Pot Ratio (SPR) determines how geometric your postflop sizing needs to be. At 100bb in a single-raised pot, SPR is roughly 11. In a 3-bet pot, SPR drops to ~4. In a 4-bet pot, SPR is ~1.

SPRTypical potGeometric river sizing
~11 (SRP)6bb33% flop → 66% turn → pot river
~4 (3BP)24bb50% flop → 75% turn → pot river
~1 (4BP)~50bb50% flop shove-equivalent

The idea: if you want to get stacks in by the river with your value hands, divide the pot into thirds and bet the stack in proportional streets. This produces natural "geometric" sizings that commit you correctly.

When to check as the PFR

You check range (or nearly range) when villain's range is equity-favored on the flop. Classic BB-defender-favored flops vs UTG open:

  • T98, 987, 876, 765 — BB has all the two-pair, straight, and set combos. UTG's AA/KK is actually behind here.
  • 654, 543, 432 (rare in practice) — pure BB flops.
  • 555, 444, 222 — paired boards where both ranges have equal sets; PFR's c-bet loses fold equity.
  • J86, 974 rainbow middling — low equity edge, villain's suited connectors thrive.

On these, check 70-100% of the time. Your bets come with strong hands that want to build the pot (like overpairs) and select combo draws, but the default is give up.

Donk betting (from OOP caller)

Historically taboo, solvers reveal that the BB donks ~10-15% of the time on specific flop textures — mainly low connected boards (654, 765, 876) where BB's range connects harder than the PFR. The donk sizing is small (25-33% pot) and the range is polar: two pair + sets + straights + flush draws, plus some backdoor bluffs.

Practical note: live pools rarely donk at all, and regs usually handle donks poorly. Implementing a donking strategy is a study priority only after the rest of your game is solid.
