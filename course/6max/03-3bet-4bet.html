3-bet, 4-bet & 5-bet Trees

The full preflop decision tree once an opener has been raised, from IP flat vs OOP 3-bet all the way to 5-bet shoves.

Linear vs polarized 3-bets

A linear 3-bet range is the top N% of hands by equity (AA, KK, QQ, AK, AQs, JJ, TT...). A polarized 3-bet range is a mix of the very best hands and hands that are too weak to flat but good as 4-bet bluffs (A5s, A4s, KJo, suited connectors that block the opener's calling range).

The rule of thumb:

  • In position, flat-calling is available → use a polarized 3-bet. The hands that play well as flats (AQo, 99, AJs, KQs) stay in your flat range where they realize equity best.
  • Out of position, flatting is weaker → use a more linear 3-bet. From the blinds especially, flats don't realize equity well due to being OOP and closing the action vs a squeeze.

The 4-bet bluff frequency

When facing a 3-bet, your 4-bet range is split between pure value (AA, KK, QQ, AKs) and 4-bet bluffs. The question every student asks: how many bluffs?

At equilibrium, you 4-bet roughly 1 bluff per 1 value when the 3-bet is OOP and geometric sizing applies. Against an IP 3-bet, drop to 1 bluff per 1.5 value because opponent's range is more condensed on value and bluffs get less fold equity.

Which bluffs? (Blocker selection)

The best 4-bet bluffs have three qualities:

  1. Block villain's value combos. A5s blocks AA, AK. KQs blocks KK, QQ.
  2. Unblock his folding region. 72o is not a 4-bet bluff — it doesn't block anything.
  3. Some equity when called. A5s has ~30% vs AA. Ideal bluff backup.

Standard 4-bet bluffs from most positions:

  • BTN vs CO 3-bet: A5s, A4s, some A3s (blocker to his 4-bet fold region KQo/AJo)
  • SB vs BTN 3-bet: A5s-A2s, some AJo and KQs mixed
  • UTG vs anyone: Rarely 4-bet bluff — range is already polar. Mostly pure value (AA/KK/QQ/AKs).

Flat-calling AA? (Sometimes yes)

A solver will flat a small percentage of AA in cold-call spots (BTN flat of UTG open, for example) to protect the rest of its flatting range. If AA is always 3-bet, then when the BTN flats, the opener knows premium hands are gone from the flat range and can attack it. A ~10% flat of AA keeps the range balanced.

Practical rule: against weak pools, don't bother. Always 3-bet AA for value. The tiny theoretical loss is dwarfed by the EV of building a bigger pot with the best hand.

5-bet shoving

When you face a 4-bet, you're usually ~25-30bb into a 100bb stack. Your shove range is heavily value-weighted: AA, KK, AKs, and a small mix of AKo. Bluff 5-bets are rare — you need huge fold equity to risk 100bb, and a 4-betting range is already narrow and bluff-resistant. A5s goes from 4-bet bluff to fold when facing a 4-bet.

Leak check: If you're 5-bet shoving QQ at 100bb, you're burning EV. QQ is a call vs 4-bet, not a shove. AA/KK/AK only for 5-bets at full stacks.
