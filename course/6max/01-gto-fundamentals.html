GTO Fundamentals & Equilibrium

What "Game Theory Optimal" really means, and why it's the right baseline to study from.

What GTO actually guarantees

A Nash equilibrium strategy guarantees exactly one thing: against a rational opponent, you cannot lose. It does not guarantee that you maximize EV against every opponent — that's what exploitative play is for. GTO is the floor. Exploits raise the ceiling.

Poker is a zero-sum game, so equilibrium strategies are symmetric in the sense that if both players play GTO, the expected value of the game equals its theoretical value (in heads-up, that's 0 for each player before rake; in multi-way spots it's your share of the positional advantage).

Minimum Defense Frequency (MDF)

When someone bets, you have to defend often enough that pure bluffs can't print. The formula is:

MDF = pot / (pot + bet)

Some common reference points:

Bet sizeMDFAlpha (fold freq we give up)
1/3 pot75%25%
1/2 pot67%33%
2/3 pot60%40%
Pot50%50%
2× pot (overbet)33%67%
Important nuance: MDF tells you the defense frequency needed to prevent a 0% equity bluff from being profitable. Solvers defend less than MDF in practice because real bluffs have some equity, and because some combos are worth more than others as bluff-catchers.

Alpha: the bluff's required fold frequency

Alpha is the mirror image of MDF — how often a pure bluff needs to work to break even.

Alpha = bet / (pot + bet)

So a pot-sized river bluff needs to succeed 50% of the time. A half-pot bluff only needs 33%. This is the clearest mathematical reason why smaller bets are better bluffs: the bar is lower.

Indifference and mixed strategies

At equilibrium the bettor is indifferent about which specific bluff he runs and the caller is indifferent about which specific bluff-catcher he calls — every candidate in each class has the same EV. If one combo had higher EV, the solver would play it more, which would shift the opponent's best response, which would drop that combo's EV back to the group average.

This is why GTO strategies are full of mixed frequencies (AsKh folds to a 3-bet 30% of the time, calls 50%, 4-bets 20%). The solver isn't hedging — it's maintaining the indifference point.

The value-to-bluff ratio

On the river, your betting range is polarized — value bets and bluffs. The ratio of value to bluffs, at equilibrium, depends on your bet size:

Bet sizeValue : Bluff (optimal)Bluffs as % of bets
1/2 pot2 : 133%
2/3 pot1.66 : 137.5%
Pot1 : 150%
2× pot1 : 267%

Memorize these. They let you reverse-engineer a hand on the fly: "villain is overbetting the river, he'd need two parts bluff per one part value — does his line have that many credible bluffs?"

Why we study GTO and not just exploits

  • Stability. Exploits that work against one player lose money against another. GTO doesn't swing with the pool.
  • Calibration. You can't identify a leak until you know what the correct frequency was. GTO is the ruler.
  • Defense against regs. Strong regs will counter-exploit you if you deviate without reason. GTO is unexploitable.
Study loop: (1) memorize the frequency, (2) understand the range it comes from, (3) deviate only when you have a concrete read justifying a shift off that frequency.
