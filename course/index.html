Course Catalog

Fifteen lessons across three formats. Work through them in order, or jump to whichever track matches the game you play most.

Track 1 — 6-max NL Cash

Six lessons. The core GTO framework expressed through the most popular online format.

  • 01GTO Fundamentals & EquilibriumMDF, alpha, indifference, and what a Nash equilibrium actually guarantees.
  • 02Preflop Opening Ranges by PositionUTG through BTN at 100bb — ranges, sizing, and why LJ is not UTG.
  • 033-bet, 4-bet & 5-bet TreesPolarized vs linear 3-bets, IP/OOP sizing, and the 4-bet bluff frequency.
  • 04Continuation Betting TheoryRange advantage, nut advantage, SPR, and small-bet vs big-bet board textures.
  • 05Turn and River PlayBarreling triple ranges, overbets, polarization and the river value-to-bluff ratio.
  • 06Blockers & Exploitative AdjustmentsHow blockers drive bluff selection and when to deviate from GTO against a live pool.

Track 2 — Heads-Up NL Cash

Four lessons. HU is where game theory becomes unavoidable.

  • 01HU Preflop: Open, Limp, 3-betThe SB's decision tree and why limping is part of a solved strategy.
  • 02HU Postflop FundamentalsSRP as BTN, SRP as BB, and the range-bet spots that dominate equilibrium play.
  • 03Polarization, Overbets & BluffcatchingWhy HU rivers get so big, and the 2:1 ratio rule for overbet shoves.
  • 04Exploitative HU AdjustmentsNode-locking typical opponent leaks: folds too much to 3-bets, overfolds turn, etc.

  • 03Stack Size StrategyDeep (40bb+), mid (20-40bb), short (10-20bb), and nit-short (≤10bb) play.
  • 04Bubble & Final TableHow pay jumps compress opening ranges and why covering stacks get to bully.
  • 05Applying ICM PressureRisk premium, FGS (Future Game Simulation), and deal-making math.
