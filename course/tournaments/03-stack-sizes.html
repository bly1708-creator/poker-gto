MTT · Lesson 03

Stack Size Strategy

Deep, mid, short, nit-short. Your effective stack dictates what game you're playing.

The four stack zones

ZoneStack (bb)Primary treeKey goal
Deep40+Open-raise + postflopAccumulate chips, implied odds matter
Mid20-40Open-raise or 3-bet shoveMaintain stack, avoid marginal spots
Short10-20Open-shove + re-shove + min-raise mixGet chips in good, preserve fold equity
Nit-short≤10Pure push/foldFind any spot with equity + fold equity

Deep (40bb+) — similar to cash, with adjustments

Deep-stacked MTT play most closely resembles 6-max cash. Open 2.2-2.5bb, defend BB wide, c-bet small on high-card flops. The key differences:

  • Less flatting of 3-bets OOP. You can't realize equity in a bloated OOP pot as well as in cash — the ICM tail starts to bite.
  • Tighter 4-bet calls. QQ becomes a mixed strategy (call/fold) vs 4-bets at 50bb effective; at 100bb it'd be a pure call.
  • Check more rivers as the PFR. Getting to showdown cheaply with medium strength is more valuable when losing a big pot means bustout.

Mid (20-40bb) — the 3-bet-or-fold zone

The signature strategy at 20-35bb is the 3-bet shove. When facing an open at this depth, flatting is weak (you create SPR you can't manage), and a small 3-bet followed by a fold to a 4-bet is disastrous. So you play a binary strategy:

  • Shove 3-bet with value + blocker bluffs (AA-88, AK-AJ, AQs, KQs, plus A2s-A5s as bluffs).
  • Flat a narrow range (small pairs, suited connectors) only when IP with implied odds.
  • Fold the rest.

30bb open-raise sizing

At 30bb, open to 2.0-2.2bb (smaller than deep). You're not building a big pot — you're stealing blinds or setting up a cheap flop. Postflop, play a tight c-bet strategy: range bet 33% on high-card dry flops, check more on wet boards.

Short (10-20bb) — the most complex zone

At 10-20bb, your tree expands to four actions: min-raise, open-shove, limp (rare), and fold. The solver's choice depends on position and stack:

PositionStackDefault action with playable hand
UTG-CO15-20bbMin-raise (2.0bb)
UTG-CO10-15bbOpen-shove premiums, min-raise others
BTN/SB12-18bbMin-raise wide (polar)
BTN/SB8-12bbOpen-shove ~50%

Facing a min-raise at 12-18bb

The ISO shove is your friend. Re-shove with 22+, suited aces, suited broadways, AQo, AJo+ depending on position. Flatting is rarely optimal because postflop with 8bb in the pot and 10bb behind, you're not going to navigate any tough decision well.

Nit-short (≤10bb) — pure push/fold

Below 10bb, you've crossed into pure Nash territory (Lesson 2). No more min-raising, no more postflop. Find your spot and shove.

Two additional nuances at 5-10bb:

  • Steal-shove. From BTN/SB with fold equity against unopened blinds, you can shove very wide (50%+ at 8bb).
  • Over-shove. Facing a min-raise, shove on top. The raiser is giving you ~3bb dead money to win; fold equity is massive.

Effective stack — it's the smaller of you and villain

Always assess the effective stack, not your own stack. If you have 40bb and villain has 10bb, you're playing a 10bb pot. That means:

  • Don't "give action" just because you're deep — villain is short, his range is polar.
  • Call shoves based on villain's stack size, not yours.
  • Don't 3-bet small and give villain the excuse to shove.

The biggest shift: players who study cash and try to apply it to MTTs get crushed in the middle stages because they keep trying to play "real poker" at 25bb. Accept that 25bb is a different game — it's mostly push/fold decorated with a few min-raises.
