Polarization, Overbets & Bluffcatching

Why HU rivers get so big, and the math behind the 2:1 overbet ratio.

Why HU polarizes more than 6-max

In a 6-max 3-bet pot, both ranges are tight and the nut distribution is fairly symmetric. In HU, preflop ranges are so wide that by the river, both players have very long tails of weak hands — missed draws, ace-highs, weak pairs. This creates ideal conditions for polarization: the bettor's strong hands extract max value while their bluffs pressure villain's bloated bluff-catcher region.

When does the solver overbet?

Three conditions must all be true:

  1. You have a nut advantage. Not just equity — specifically the hands in the top 5% of the board's equity distribution.
  2. Villain is capped. His range can't contain the absolute top of hands (or contains them only rarely).
  3. Stacks allow threatening action. An overbet that commits <70% of stacks is a strong lever. One that's only 10bb into 50bb is just noise.

The classic HU overbet spot: BTN vs BB, static turn

SB opens, BB calls. Flop K♠7♦2♣, BB check-calls 33% c-bet. Turn Q♥. SB's range now contains every strong combo (KQ, KK, QQ, AK, AA) that BB doesn't have — BB would have 3-bet those. SB's solver action: overbet ~125% pot with a polar range of KQ+ as value and A5s/A4s blocker bluffs.

Overbet math — why 2:1 bluffs to value

Recall from 6-max Lesson 1: the value-to-bluff ratio depends on bet size. At 2× pot:

Alpha = bet / (pot + bet) = 2 / (1 + 2) = 67%
Villain must defend 33%. Your range: 33% value, 67% bluff → 1:2 ratio.

That means for every value combo in your overbet range, you need two bluff combos. This is why overbetting is not a casual decision — you need enough credible bluffs to justify the big size, or villain over-folds and the bet is exploitable.

Overbet shoves (all-in river)

When stacks are shallow enough that an overbet shove makes sense (typically SPR < 1.5 on the river), the value/bluff math gets cleaner because shoves have no meta. Calculate the implied ratio from the pot-to-shove size.

Bluffcatching HU overbets

When villain overbets, you must defend MDF with your strongest bluff-catchers. Against a 2× overbet you defend 33%. Selection criteria:

  • Blockers to villain's value. On a Q-high board where villain overbets, KQ (blocker to QQ) beats most of his bluffs and blocks one of the main value combos.
  • Unblockers to villain's bluffs. You want missed draws to still be in his range, so avoid hands that hold his air cards.
  • Minimum equity to call. Against a 2× overbet you need pot odds of 29%. Verify your hand has at least that against villain's implied range.

When to check rather than overbet

You do not overbet every node where you have nut advantage. You check when:

  • Villain's range already folds too much — smaller sizing extracts more EV.
  • You don't have enough credible bluffs to balance (usually because your range is condensed on value).
  • Your range includes medium-strength hands that prefer smaller bets (pocket nines overbetting into a potential K-high is a leak).
Mental model: the bigger your bet, the more polarized your range must be. Your medium-strength hands stay in the check line. Your nuts and your air go to the big-bet line. If you can't cleanly split those groups, you're betting the wrong size.
