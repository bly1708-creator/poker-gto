HU Postflop Fundamentals

Range bets, defense frequencies, and why the HU SB c-bets flops at frequencies that look insane to a 6-max player.

HU flops favor the raiser more than 6-max

The SB open range (~75%) is much wider than a 6-max CO/BTN open, but the BB call range is also wider (~60%). Despite that symmetry, the SB's range is linear and capped differently: SB's range excludes the very bottom trash, while BB's range excludes premiums (they 3-bet instead). The result: SB has a range advantage on most high-card flops and roughly neutral equity on low/connected boards.

SB as the PFR: high c-bet frequencies

In HU single-raised pots at 100bb, SB's c-bet frequency at solver equilibrium is often 70-100% on high-card flops, using a small (25-33%) size. This looks aggressive but is correct because:

  • SB has all the AA/KK/QQ combos that BB's calling range cannot have (BB 3-bet them).
  • BB is capped at roughly AQ/KQ as "nut" hands on an A-K-x board.
  • Small sizing denies equity to BB's underpairs (22-99 on A-high) cheaply.

BB defense vs small c-bets

When SB bets 33% pot, BB's MDF is 75% — a very wide defense number. Practically:

  • Call any pair, any gutshot, any overcard with backdoors.
  • Fold only completely airy hands with no backdoor equity (9-high no draw on A-K-4).
  • Check-raise ~10% — polar: two pair+ plus select combo draws as bluffs.

Check-raise sizing in HU

BB's check-raises against small SB c-bets run ~3.5-4× the bet (to ~12-15bb into an 8bb pot). The polar construction:

  • Value: sets, two pair, top pair top kicker sometimes.
  • Bluffs: gutshots, open-enders, flush draws with backdoor additions, combo bluffs.
  • Ratio: 1:1 value to bluff at flop — the bluffs have equity to realize.

SRP as BB (when SB limps)

In HU limped pots, neither range has a significant equity advantage — both are wide, SB's limp range is polar, BB checked its option range. Postflop play becomes about texture:

  • BB often leads 25-33% on flops where its range connects harder (paired boards, low connected).
  • Check-check is common — both players navigate to the turn before committing.
  • SB's stabs after check are small and wide (30-40% when bet).

3-bet pots: smaller ranges, steeper decisions

In a BB 3-bet pot, SPR is ~4. BB has the nut advantage (he 3-bet with the premiums SB doesn't have). But SB has position, which partially offsets.

BB's 3-bet pot c-bet strategy

  • A-high, K-high dry: Range bet small (33% pot). Bets 80%+ of range.
  • Middling connected (T98, 876): Check ~60%. Polar when betting (75%).
  • Paired: Bet small, ~80% frequency.

SB's response

  • Vs small c-bet: defend ~75% (call wide, raise narrow and polar).
  • Vs big c-bet: defend ~60%, raise less often, fold more.
  • SB raise sizing: ~3× the c-bet, preserving SPR for a committed turn.
Key mental model: in HU, you can't get away from a made hand as cleanly as in 6-max. Ranges are wide, bluffs are credible at every street, and overfolds are the #1 leak in the population. When your MDF says call, call.
