Heads-Up · Lesson 01

HU Preflop: Open, Limp, 3-bet

In heads-up, the small blind is the button. Every hand starts with a big decision.

SB as button: a very different environment

In 6-max, the SB is the second-worst position (BB is worst). In heads-up, the SB is the button — it acts last preflop (after BB's action) and gets position for the entire hand. This makes the SB the biggest winner in HU, full stop.

Because of that positional edge, the SB plays a very wide range: ~80% of hands at 100bb. A tight SB is a losing SB in HU.

The three-action tree at 100bb

The solver's SB strategy mixes three actions:

  • Min-raise (2bb): ~70% of hands. A small, wide open.
  • Limp (1bb): ~10% of hands. A polar mix — strong and speculative.
  • Fold: ~20% of hands. The worst offsuit garbage (92o, 83o, 72o, 62o, 52o, 42o, 32o, and similar).

Why limping exists in solver HU

Limping has two functions at equilibrium:

  1. Weak hands that can realize equity cheaply. K5o, Q7o, J8o — too weak to raise-and-fold profitably, too strong to give up.
  2. Strong hands to protect the limp range. A sprinkle of premiums (AA, AK) in the limp range prevents BB from attacking it with big raises.
Should you limp in practice? Against a solver BB, yes — the EV gain is real. Against a human BB who over-raises limps or never checks back premium hands, pure min-raise with a 75% linear range is simpler and often more profitable. Start simple; add limping after 100k hands of volume.

BB's response to SB min-raise

BB faces a 2bb open with 1bb already posted and ~1bb equity from position reversal when OOP. The solver's BB strategy:

ActionFrequencyRange
Fold~25%Weakest offsuit: 82o, 72o, 62o, 52o, 42o, 32o, 92o mostly
Call~60%The vast middle: pocket pairs, suited cards, most broadways, suited connectors
3-bet~15%Polar: premiums (TT+, AK, AQs) + bluffs (A2s-A5s, 54s, some suited broadway mixes)

BB 3-bet sizing: 3.5× to 4×

Because the BB is OOP postflop, it uses a larger 3-bet size (3.5-4×) to (a) build a pot where its value hands dominate and (b) deny SB a cheap flat with hands like 65s or K7s that play well IP.

SB's response to BB 3-bet

At 100bb, facing a 4× 3-bet from BB, SB's options:

  • 4-bet for value: AA, KK, some QQ, AKs.
  • 4-bet as bluff: A5s-A2s (blocker bluffs).
  • Call: Middle pairs (22-JJ), most broadways, suited connectors, suited aces that don't make the bluff cut.
  • Fold: The bottom of the raising range — 83s, 74o, 42s, etc.

The 4-bet sizing is ~2.3× the 3-bet (so ~9bb over a 3.5× to 4× 3-bet). This leaves a clean SPR for postflop play in 4-bet pots.

SB limp-then-raise trees

When SB limps and BB raises (isoing the limp), SB faces a 3-way decision:

  • Fold the bottom of the limp range (J8o, K5o) to larger iso sizes.
  • Call most of the middle.
  • 3-bet limp-raise with strongest limp combos (AA, KK that were limped for balance) plus select bluffs.
Common leak: BB raises every SB limp blindly. If SB's limp range is balanced, this is -EV. Raise only when your hand actually beats the limp range's equity, or limp-check back in BB (yes, this is a solver option too) to realize equity cheaply.

Deep stacks (200bb+)

As stacks deepen, SB's RFI widens slightly (hitting ~85%+ at 200bb) but sizing increases (2.5× or 3× opens) because implied odds let hands like suited connectors play for more. BB 3-bet sizing also grows (to ~4.5×). The structural strategy — polar 3-bets, blocker-driven 4-bets — stays the same.
